APPENDIX II. Notes on copper-plate Grants in the possession of the Srirangam Temple.



ARIE/1887-1888/II//1 (page 6)
Remarks
A grant of three plates. The seal contains a boar, the sun and the moon. The grant was issued during the reign of Krishnaraya of Vijayanagara in Saka 1436, Bhava.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//2 (page 6)
Remarks
A grant of five plates with ring, but without seal. It belongs to the reign of Viradeva-raya.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//3 (page 6)
Remarks
Three plates without seal. Devaraya of Vijayanagara, Saka 1356, Ananda.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//4 (page 6)
Remarks
Three plates. Seal with boar, sun and moon. Krishnaraya of Vijayanagara, Saka 1450 (?) Sarvajit.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//5 (page 6)
Remarks
Three plates which are strung together with an odd Vijayanagara plate of larger size. Ring without seal. Devaraya of Vijayanagara, Plavamga year.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//6 (page 6)
Remarks
Three plates without ring and seal. Saka 1383, Chitrabhanu.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//7 (page 6)
Remarks
Three plates without ring and seal. Saka 1343.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//8 and 9 (page 6)
Remarks
These two numbers consist of three and five large plates with ring respectively and contain Telugu songs.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//10 (page 6)
Remarks
One plate. It is a sale-deed (krayasasana) and dated in the Krodhana year.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//11 (page 6)
Remarks
One plate. Saka 1210, Kilaka.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//12 (page 6)
Remarks
One plate. Saka 1614, Srimukha.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//13 (page 6)
Remarks
Two plates in the Nandinagari character without ring and seal.
ARIE/1887-1888/II//14 (page 6)
Remarks
Five plates with ring, but without seal. Saka 1270.
