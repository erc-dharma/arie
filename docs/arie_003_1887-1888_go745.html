II.—CHIDAMBARAM.



ARIE/1887-1888/I/1887-188802/115 (page 4)
Place
Outside first prakara, east
Remarks
Two Sanskrit verses, which praise the victories of Kulottunga-Chola over the five Pandyas and the Keralas, and which record that he burnt the fort of Korgara and that he put up a pillar of victory on a peak of the Sahyadri mountains.
ARIE/1887-1888/I/1887-188802/116 (page 4)
Place
Mandapa in front of the east entrance into the first prakara.
Remarks
Sanskrit fragment.
ARIE/1887-1888/I/1887-188802/117 (page 4)
Place
Outside first prakara, north
Remarks
Dated in the 46th year of Kulottunga-Sora-deva; mentions Madu-rantaki, “the younger sister of our lord.” Sirrambalam is here also called Puliyur.
ARIE/1887-1888/I/1887-188802/118 (page 4)
Place
Do. do.
Remarks
Ko-Rajakesarivarman, alias Udaiyar Rajendra-Sora-deva, 24th year.
ARIE/1887-1888/I/1887-188802/119 (page 4)
Place
Do. do.
Remarks
The inscription records several gifts to the god (Aludaiyar) of Chidam-baram (Tillai or Tiruchchirrambalam) by Rajarajan-Kundavai, the younger sister of Tribhuvanachakravartin Kulottunga-Sora-deva. Further it records, that Rajendra-Sora-deva put up at the temple a stone, which he had received from the king of Kamboja.
ARIE/1887-1888/I/1887-188802/120 (page 4)
Place
Do. west
Remarks
Long defaced inscription in Sanskrit verse.
ARIE/1887-1888/I/1887-188802/121 (page 4)
Place
Inside second prakara, west, left of entrance.
Remarks
Ko-Parakesarivarman, alias Tribhuvanachakravartin .... The inscription, which is much worn, mentions Ammangai, the daughter of Kulottunga-Sora-deva.
